Corn-based PP utilizes starch to create biodegradable plastics, offering a sustainable alternative to fossil fuels. Sugarcane-derived PP, often labeled as "green," is produced through fermentation processes, promoting lower carbon emissions. Similarly, beet-based PP harnesses the sugars present in sugar beets, contributing to eco-friendly packaging solutions. These bio-based sources enhance sustainability in the polypropylene market, reducing reliance on traditional petrochemical routes.</p></p>
