<p><p>The food grade thickener market encompasses three primary types: synthetic polymers, natural polymers, and their derivatives. Synthetic polymers, such as xanthan gum and carrageenan, are chemically produced to enhance texture and stability in food products. Natural polymers, derived from plants or animals, include guar gum and agar, offering clean-label appeal due to their origins. Derivatives combine properties of both, serving specific functional roles in food applications. Together, these thickeners improve viscosity, mouthfeel, and overall product quality.</p></p>
