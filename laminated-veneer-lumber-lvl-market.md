<p><p>Laminated Veneer Lumber (LVL) is an engineered wood product made by bonding layers of thin wood veneers with adhesives. The LVL market is divided into two primary types: structural and non-structural. Structural LVL is used in load-bearing applications, such as beams, headers, and joists, offering high strength and stability. Non-structural LVL, on the other hand, is utilized for aesthetic features, furniture, and interior applications, focusing more on appearance and design than structural integrity, thus catering to diverse construction and design needs.</p></p>
